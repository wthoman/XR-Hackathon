## The Spectacles Community Challenge is OPEN! 🙌

We're excited to launch a brand new virtual hackathon series – Spectacles Community Challenges. Each month brings fresh opportunities to explore the cutting-edge AR creation format on Spectacles, or dive deeper into working with this top-notch AR device and win up to $5,000! 😎

Whether you're creating immersive games, practical utilities, or experimental experiences, the Spectacles Community Challenge offers a platform to showcase your innovation and creativity while connecting with a global community of AR developers.

<CollapsibleSection title="Judging Criteria" defaultOpen={false} level={3}>
  <p>
    Submissions are evaluated by a panel of experts from the Snap AR team based
    on the following criteria:
  </p>

<h4>New Lens & Lens Update Categories</h4>
<p>
  <strong>Engagement metrics:</strong>
</p>
<ul>
  <li>Unique Users</li>
  <li>Lens Play Time</li>
</ul>
<p>
  <strong>Lens quality:</strong>
</p>
<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>User Experience</strong> - The Lens is intuitive to use, easy to
    navigate, and delivers user value. Interactions feel natural and responsive.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Completeness & Depth</strong> - The Lens feels complete and provides
    recurring value. If there is storytelling, there is a beginning, middle and
    end, and a way to repeat.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Design & Polish</strong> - The Lens is visually appealing and
    polished, with attention to detail in both aesthetics and functionality.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Retentive Value</strong> - The Lens has mechanisms that encourage
    users to return to the Lens over time.
  </li>
</ul>

  <h4>Open Source Category</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <strong>Project Setup Quality</strong> - Clear project structure, proper dependencies, and easy setup instructions
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Code Quality</strong> - Clean, well-organized, and maintainable code with proper commenting
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Documentation Quality</strong> - Comprehensive README, clear installation steps, and usage examples
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Uniqueness</strong> - Original concepts that didn't simply recycle existing sample projects and reuse Asset Library assets
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Completeness & Depth</strong> - Feature-complete projects that demonstrate thorough implementation
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Design & Polish</strong> - Visually appealing and polished user experience with attention to detail
    </li>
  </ul>
</CollapsibleSection>

<CollapsibleSection title="FAQ" defaultOpen={false} level={3}>
  <p>
    <strong>Q: Do I need a pair of Spectacles to participate?</strong>
  </p>
  <p>
    A: While having Spectacles is beneficial for testing your experiences, you
    can develop using Lens Studio's device simulator. However, your submission
    should be designed specifically for Spectacles.
  </p>

{' '}

<p>
  <strong>Q: Can I submit multiple projects?</strong>
</p>
<p>
  A: Yes, you can submit multiple projects across different categories, but each
  project can only be submitted to one category.
</p>

{' '}

<p>
  <strong>Q: What happens if I win?</strong>
</p>
<p>
  A: Winners receive prizes, recognition in our developer documentation,
  promotion through Snap's developer channels, and the opportunity to engage
  with Snap's AR team for feedback.
</p>

  <p>
    <strong>Q: Can I submit a project that uses experimental APIs?</strong>
  </p>
  <p>
    A: Yes! Projects using experimental features should be submitted to the Open
    Source category where they can be evaluated based on their innovation and
    technical implementation.
  </p>
</CollapsibleSection>
